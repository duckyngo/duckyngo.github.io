📜 Thân Thế & Lai Lịch

Vũ Ảo Thuật lần đầu trải nghiệm VR khi dùng Oculus DK1 — resolution thấp, screen door effect, và ông nôn sau 10 phút. Nhưng khoảnh khắc đầu tiên nhìn xung quanh trong virtual world, ông biết: đây là tương lai. Ông đã develop cho mọi platform XR: Oculus/Meta Quest, HTC Vive, PlayStation VR, HoloLens, Apple Vision Pro, Magic Leap. Mỗi platform có quirks riêng: tracking quality, field of view, input methods, performance constraints. Ông đã chứng kiến: CEO nôn trong VR demo (bad day for the pitch), AR marker tracking fail trong ánh sáng mạnh (demo thất bại tại outdoor event), và hand tracking detect bàn tay giả (mannequin hand triggered interaction). Motto: 'XR development = game development + perception science + UX design + hardware optimization. Drop bất kỳ thứ nào = bad experience = motion sickness = user never returns.'

🗝️ Bí Kíp

Bí Kíp #1: Motion Sickness — Kẻ Thù Số 1

Nguyên nhân: mismatch giữa visual motion và vestibular (inner ear) sensation. Mắt thấy di chuyển, tai nói đứng yên → brain confused → nausea. Giải pháp: 90fps MINIMUM (Quest 2: 72/90/120Hz), low latency (motion-to-photon < 20ms), KHÔNG move camera mà player không expect. Locomotion: teleport (safest), snap turn (thay smooth turn), vignette khi di chuyển (reduce peripheral vision). ĐỪNG BAO GIỜ move camera bằng code mà không phải player-initiated. Test với motion-sensitive users — nếu họ OK, most users will be fine.

Bí Kíp #2: Spatial Computing — Thiết Kế 3D UI

2D UI rules KHÔNG áp dụng cho 3D. Buttons phải đủ lớn cho hand/controller targeting. Text readable ở distance (minimum angular size). World-locked vs head-locked UI: world-locked (stays in place, more immersive) vs head-locked (follows head, easier to find but immersion-breaking). Diegetic UI: UI tích hợp vào game world (HP hiển thị trên cánh tay nhân vật). Non-diegetic: floating panels. Depth: UI elements ở comfortable focal distance (1-3m). Avoid UI < 0.5m (eye strain) hoặc > 5m (hard to read).

Bí Kíp #3: Hand Tracking — Beyond Controllers

Meta Quest hand tracking: 6DOF per hand, finger joint tracking, pinch/grab gestures. Apple Vision Pro: eye tracking + hand pinch = primary input. Challenges: occlusion (hands block each other), fast movement blur, varied hand sizes. Design: generous hit targets, visual feedback khi hand near interactive element, haptic-less interaction (no physical feedback → need strong visual/audio feedback). Fallback: luôn support controllers alongside hand tracking (hand tracking reliability < 100%).

Bí Kíp #4: Performance Optimization cho XR

VR renders STEREO (2 eyes) = double rendering cost. 90fps = 11ms per frame budget (cả 2 eyes). Foveated rendering: full resolution center (where eyes look), lower resolution peripheral → 30-50% performance gain. Fixed Foveated (Quest 2), Eye-tracked Foveated (Quest Pro, Vision Pro). Application SpaceWarp (ASW) / reprojection: khi frame miss, synthesize frame từ previous + motion data — safety net nhưng visible artifacts. Single-pass stereo rendering: render both eyes in single pass → reduce CPU overhead.

Bí Kíp #5: AR Tracking & Anchors

SLAM (Simultaneous Localization And Mapping): camera track position in real world. Plane detection: identify floors, walls, tables. Light estimation: match virtual lighting to real environment. Anchors: attach virtual objects to real-world positions (persist across sessions). Cloud anchors: shared AR experience between devices. Challenges: tracking loss (fast movement, textureless surfaces, reflective surfaces), scale accuracy, outdoor lighting. ARKit (iOS) vs ARCore (Android) vs OpenXR: platform-specific nhưng converging on OpenXR standard.

🌙 Chuyện Kể Đêm Khuya

📖 Chuyện 1: VR Demo Khiến CEO Nôn — Worst Pitch Ever

Startup demo VR product cho investor. CEO đeo headset, demo starts: smooth camera animation fly through virtual office. 30 giây → CEO mặt xanh. 60 giây → CEO giơ tay ra hiệu dừng. 90 giây → CEO tháo headset, chạy ra toilet. Vấn đề: smooth camera movement (cinematic) = worst possible VR locomotion. Vestibular system: 'Tôi KHÔNG di chuyển!' Visual system: 'Chúng ta ĐANG bay!' Brain: *nôn*. Demo fail, investment round fail, startup pivot. Post-mortem: team test VR trên developer có 'VR legs' (quen VR) — không ai sick. Nhưng first-time VR users (most investors) extremely sensitive. Rule learned: TELEPORT locomotion cho demos, NGẮN (< 3 phút), headset sạch (hygiene = first impression).

💡 Bài học: VR demos cho non-gamers/first-timers: 1) TELEPORT locomotion only, 2) SHORT experience (2-3 minutes), 3) NO camera movement by code, 4) Static scene preferred, 5) Clean headset (face foam replacement between users), 6) Monitor user comfort actively (ask every 30 seconds). Test demo with VR-naive users specifically — your team has VR legs, investors don't.

📖 Chuyện 2: AR Marker Tracking Fail — Outdoor Demo Disaster

AR app for outdoor event: scan poster → 3D model appears. Indoor testing: perfect tracking, model stable. Event day (outdoor, sunny): camera overexposed → marker barely visible → tracking jittery → 3D model jumps around → client disappointed. Worse: strong sunlight washes out phone screen → user can't see AR overlay. Wind blows poster → marker moves → tracking lost entirely. Rain → poster wet → marker distorted → no tracking. Fix: 1) Markerless AR (surface detection instead of marker) for outdoor, 2) High-contrast marker design (robust to lighting), 3) GPS/compass-based AR for outdoor (less precise but weather-independent), 4) Backup: QR code → web-based 3D viewer (no AR tracking needed).

💡 Bài học: AR in uncontrolled environments (outdoor, events, retail) = CHALLENGING. Marker-based AR: fails in bright sun, rain, wind. Alternatives: markerless (plane detection), location-based (GPS), LiDAR-based (iPhone Pro). ALWAYS have fallback for when tracking fails. Test in TARGET environment conditions, not just office.

📖 Chuyện 3: Hand Tracking Detect Bàn Tay Mannequin

VR retail experience: customer use hand tracking để interact với virtual products. Store has mannequins near VR station. Hand tracking detect mannequin hands as real hands → phantom interactions → virtual objects moving by themselves → users freaked out: 'The mannequin is using VR!' Funnier: employee's hand gesture while talking near headset → accidentally trigger purchase button → order placed → billing confusion. Fix: 1) Hand tracking confidence threshold (reject low-confidence detections), 2) Require intentional gesture (pinch + hold, not just proximity), 3) Interaction zones (only detect hands within defined area), 4) Move mannequins away from VR stations.

💡 Bài học: Hand tracking false positives: non-hand objects detected as hands, unintentional gestures trigger actions. Design defensive interactions: 1) Require intentional gestures (not just presence), 2) Confirmation for critical actions (double-tap, hold), 3) Confidence threshold filtering, 4) Spatial bounds for interaction zones. Test with environmental clutter — real deployments have mannequins, passing people, pets.

💀 Sai Lầm Kinh Điển

❌ Sai Lầm #1: Target < 90fps cho VR

72fps minimum (Quest 2 supports 72/90/120). Below 72fps = motion sickness for most users. Budget: 11ms per frame for 90fps. Profile GPU: mỗi ms matters. Reprojection là safety net, không phải target.

❌ Sai Lầm #2: Smooth locomotion as default

Smooth continuous movement = nausea cho 40% users. Default: teleport + snap turn. Option: smooth locomotion cho experienced users. Comfort settings ACCESSIBLE in menu, not hidden.

❌ Sai Lầm #3: UI text quá nhỏ

VR headset resolution per degree < monitor. Text must be LARGER. Minimum: 1° angular height (roughly arm's length reading). Test readability trên actual headset, not in Unity editor.

❌ Sai Lầm #4: Ignore hand hygiene for shared headsets

VR headset touches face → bacteria, sweat, makeup. Shared headsets (demo, arcade): disposable face covers, cleaning protocol between users. COVID amplified this concern. No hygiene = no repeat users.

❌ Sai Lầm #5: Không test với người mới dùng VR

Developer team quen VR → 'VR legs' → comfortable with everything. Real users: first time = fragile. Test tutorial/onboarding với VR virgins. Every discomfort they feel = design problem to solve.
